Why it matters

How social actually grows the business

It rarely closes a sale on its own — but skip it, and every other channel gets more expensive.

Builds the awareness ads pay for

Consistent posting keeps you visible between ad flights, so paid traffic isn't meeting your brand for the first time.

Builds trust before the sale

People check your profile before they buy. An active, responsive account is often the deciding factor, not the ad itself.

Feeds cheaper retargeting

Every engaged viewer becomes a warm audience — retargeting ads to people who already know you cost less and convert more.

Turns customers into a channel

Shares, tags, and comments put you in front of your customers' networks — reach paid media can't buy on its own.

A live read on what customers want

Comments and DMs are direct feedback — on offers, objections, and messaging — before you spend more on ads to find out.

Feeds SEO and AEO too

Social profiles rank, get cited, and reinforce the same facts about your business that search engines and AI answers pull from.

Where it sits in the system: social isn't a separate line item — it's the retention and advocacy stages of your funnel, running every day, feeding warmer audiences back into paid and search.

How customers find you

Search is splitting in two — SEO and AEO

People still type into Google. But more of them are now asking ChatGPT, Perplexity, and AI overviews for a direct answer instead of a list of links to click through. Ranking for both is quickly becoming table stakes, not a nice-to-have.

SEO — search engine optimization

Getting your website to rank higher in traditional search results, so people searching for what you offer find you before they find a competitor.

  • Keyword research and content strategy
  • Technical SEO and site speed
  • On-page optimization
  • Backlinks and domain authority
  • Local SEO for physical locations

AEO — answer engine optimization

Getting your brand mentioned and cited directly inside AI-generated answers — on tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's AI overviews — where there's no list of links, just one answer.

  • Clear, structured, directly-answerable content
  • Schema and structured data markup
  • Authoritative, citable brand mentions
  • FAQ and comparison-style content
  • Consistent facts about your business across the web

Why this matters now: search is no longer one channel. SEO wins the click. AEO wins the mention when there's no click to win at all. Businesses optimizing for only one are already behind.
